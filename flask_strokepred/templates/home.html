Understanding Stroke and Prevention

What is a Stroke?

A stroke occurs when the blood supply to part of the brain is interrupted or reduced, depriving brain tissue of oxygen and nutrients. This can result in damage to brain cells and loss of function.

Best Practices for Stroke Prevention

  • Manage blood pressure
  • Control cholesterol levels
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Exercise regularly
  • Eat a healthy diet low in saturated fat and cholesterol
  • Limit alcohol intake
  • Don't smoke

Additional Information

Stroke is a leading cause of disability and death worldwide. Recognizing the symptoms and taking preventive measures can significantly reduce the risk of stroke.

Types of Stroke

Ischemic stroke: The most common type, caused by a blockage in an artery leading to the brain.

Hemorrhagic stroke: Less common, caused by bleeding in the brain.
